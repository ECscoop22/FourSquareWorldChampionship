Rules of Four Square

📜 Introduction

The Four Square World Championships is an annual tournament held in Bridgton, Maine, to crown the world champions of the sport. This rulebook serves as the official guide governing gameplay, court specifications, and tournament structure.

🏐 Equipment

Ball: An 8.5-inch rubber playground ball inflated to 2 lbs.

🟦 Court Specifications

Dimensions: A 16 x 16-foot square divided into four equal 8 x 8-foot squares, numbered 1–4 with 4 and 1 diagonal from each other.

Lines:

  • All court lines are 1 inch wide.
  • Outside lines are in-bounds; a ball landing on these remains in play.
  • Inside lines (dividing squares) are out-of-bounds.

🚫 Out-of-Play Zone

To balance overhand and closed-fist strikes, the Out-of-Play Zone discourages unreturnable shots:

  • A 10-foot zone beyond each court boundary.
  • If a ball lands here after a legal hit, the striker is out.
  • Used in the Championship Round based on venue/house rules.

🎯 Objective

Be the last player standing! Each round begins with one player per square and others in line. The player in Square 4 serves to Square 1. Play continues until someone is out.

👣 Player Positioning

  • Square 4 serves to Square 1.
  • Players may move freely without interference.

📤 Serving the Ball

  • Adults: must serve within 6 inches of the back corner (1 foot for kids).
  • Serve must bounce and be hit with one/two hands to Square 1 — not above head height.
  • Player in Square 1 gets one "one bad" do-over.

✋ Ball Striking Rules

  • Only hands/wrists may strike the ball.
  • No carrying, holding, or double taps.
  • Unnatural spins are allowed if hit with one clean strike.

🙅 Interference & Poaching

  • Only the square’s owner may hit the ball after a bounce.
  • Any contact by others = out if the owner made an attempt and had a chance to return it.
  • No physical blocking of opponents.
  • Kids' divisions require a bounce before hitting.

❌ Elimination (Out)

  • Letting the ball bounce twice.
  • Hitting it out-of-bounds or on inside lines.
  • Carrying, poaching, interfering.

🏟️ Court Environment

  • Ball bouncing off walls = in play.
  • If a player had a legit chance and it hits a chair or person: round restarts.
  • If no chance to return it: player is out.

⚔️ Showdown Rule

Disputes? Settle it with a 1-on-1 showdown using just 2 squares. Higher square serves.

🚫 Prohibited Moves

  • Blackjack: Catching the ball.
  • Spinzies: Using two hands to spin.
  • Short-stopping: Hitting ball to self.
  • Body Language: Using body parts other than hands.

👫 Divisions

  • Boys 14 & under
  • Girls 14 & under
  • Men 15+
  • Women 15+

🏆 Tournament Structure

Round 1 (Scoring Round):

  • Kids play together; adults separated by gender.
  • 45 minutes of play across multiple courts.
  • Each "out" is tallied.

Round 2 (Semifinals):

  • Divisions separated again — 25 minutes of play.
  • “Outs” continue from Round 1.
  • Top 8 players per division advance.

Championship Round:

  • Finalists compete in a “10-out” untimed final.
  • Square 2 is vacated with 3 players left.
  • 2 remaining players go 1v1 in adjacent squares.
  • Last player standing is crowned champion.
