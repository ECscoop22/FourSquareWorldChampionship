Rules of Four Square

Introduction

The Four Square World Championships is an annual tournament held in Bridgton, Maine, to crown the world champions of the sport. This rulebook serves as the official guide governing gameplay, court specifications, and tournament structure.

Equipment

Ball: An 8.5-inch rubber playground ball inflated to 2 lbs.

Court Specifications

Dimensions: A 16 x 16-foot square divided into four equal 8 x 8-foot squares, numbered 1–4 with 4 and 1 diagonal from each other.

4   3
2   1

Lines:

  • All court lines are 1 inch wide.
  • Outside lines are in-bounds.
  • Inside lines are out-of-bounds.

Out-of-Play Zone

  • 10 ft boundary around the court.
  • If a legally hit ball lands in the Out-of-Play Zone after bouncing in another square, the striker is out.
  • Only used in the Championship Round, depending on venue.

Objective

Be the last remaining player. Square 4 serves to Square 1. Rally continues until someone is out.

Player Positioning

  • Each square has one player.
  • Square 4 serves to Square 1.
  • Players can move freely but may not interfere.

Serving the Ball

  • Server (Square 4) stands near corner and bounces/hits to Square 1.
  • Serve must be non-aggressive and below head height.
  • One do-over allowed ("one bad").
  • Illegal serve = server out.

Ball Striking Rules

  • Strike with hand(s) only.
  • No catching, holding, or carrying.
  • Only one deliberate hit allowed per strike.
  • Unnatural spins allowed as long as they follow hit rules.

Interference, Poaching & Possession

  • Only the player whose square the ball bounces in can hit it.
  • If another player hits it first and the correct player had a chance to play, the poacher is out.
  • If no attempt was made, the player of that square is out.
  • No physical obstruction allowed.

Elimination ("Out")

  • Fails to return ball correctly.
  • Double bounce.
  • Hits out or on inside line.
  • Illegal hits or poaching.

Court Environment

  • If ball hits wall after bounce — continue play.
  • If ball hits chair/person and player had a chance — dead ball, replay.
  • If player couldn’t return anyway — they’re out.

Showdown Rule

Players may settle disputes with a 1-on-1 match in two squares. Higher square serves.

Prohibited Moves

  • Blackjack: Catching the ball outside squares.
  • Spinzies: Two-handed spin hits.
  • Short-stopping: Hitting to self.
  • Body language: Hitting with body instead of hands.

Divisions

  • Children’s Girls: 14 & under
  • Children’s Boys: 14 & under
  • Women: 15+
  • Men: 15+

Tournament Structure

Round 1 (Scoring):

  • Children together on 4 courts for 45 minutes.
  • Men and Women separately for 45 minutes on 2 courts.
  • “Outs” are tracked.

Round 2 (Semifinals):

  • 25 minutes each division. Players continue from Round 1.
  • Top 8 lowest “outs” advance.

Championship Round:

  • Untimed “10-out” format.
  • Start position drawn from hat.
  • 3 players left → Square 2 removed. 2 players → 1v1 in diagonal squares.
  • Last player wins the championship!
